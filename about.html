Why Happy Hour in Costa Rica?

Costa Rica's happy hour scene is legendary. When the sun sets in Costa Rica, the country comes alive - from vibrant bars and clubs to laid-back beachside settings. Enjoying a drink with friends is central to the "Pura Vida" lifestyle.

The National Spirit: Guaro (brand name: Cacique) is Costa Rica's most popular liquor - a clear sugarcane spirit similar to vodka but naturally sweeter. Try it in a Guaro Sour (with lime and sugar) or the famous Chiliguaro shot (guaro + hot sauce + lime + salt) born in the bars of Playas del Coco.

Local Beers: Imperial (since 1924, with its iconic black eagle logo) and Pilsen (since 1888) dominate. Light and refreshing, perfect for tropical weather. A growing craft beer scene features Costa Rica Craft Brewing's Libertas and Segua, plus Monteverde Brewing Co using local mineral-rich waters.

Happy Hour Culture: Most bars offer happy hour from 4-6pm or 4:30-6:30pm with 2-for-1 deals on cocktails and $2-4 beers. Beachfront spots like Tamarindo's Langosta Beach Club and Manuel Antonio's El Avion (built inside a historic cargo plane) make sunset watching an art form.
