Costa Rica's Happy Hour Hotspots

From bustling cities to serene beaches, Costa Rica offers incredible happy hour experiences

San Jose

The capital's most popular neighborhoods for happy hour include Barrio Escalante and La California, filled with modern bars, craft breweries, and fine dining restaurants. Club Vertigo offers world-class DJs spinning techno and house, while Jazz Cafe sets the standard for live music concerts.

Must-visit spots: Costa Rica Beer Factory (craft beers brewed on-site), The Public House (hipster craft cocktails), El Steinvorth & Antik (live music and DJ sets), The Craic Irish Pub (multiple stouts on tap), and Selvatica (trendy rooftop with sunset views).

Jaco

Costa Rica's most energetic coastal town, just 90 minutes from San Jose. The main strip is a neon-lit corridor of bars, clubs, and casinos creating non-stop energy until the early hours. Perfect for bachelor parties, surfers, and anyone who wants to party hard.

Must-visit spots: Le Loft (top nightclub for 10+ years), Backyard Bar (famous Happy Hour with live music), Clarita's (beachfront legend with sunset views), PuddleFish Brewery (craft beer and fish tacos), Koko (laid-back beachfront at golden hour), and Republik Lounge (creative cocktails and DJ sets).

Tamarindo

A vibrant beach town on Costa Rica's Pacific coast, famous for stunning sunsets and lively happy hour specials. The town comes alive as the sun dips below the horizon, with beachfront bars offering 2-for-1 deals and $2 beers while you watch the sky turn orange and pink.

Must-visit spots: El Chiringuito ($2 beers at sunset), Volcano Brewing Company (2x1 craft beers 4-7pm), Nogui's (2-for-1 cocktails since 1974), Pangas (feet-in-sand happy hour 4:30-6:30pm), Langosta Beach Club (half-priced drinks 4-6pm), and Pacifico Bar (Wild Nights on Wednesday/Saturday).

Manuel Antonio

Where rainforest meets the Pacific, Manuel Antonio offers happy hour with panoramic views. Bars perched on hillsides overlook the national park and ocean, creating a magical setting for sunset cocktails alongside monkeys and toucans.

Must-visit spots: El Avion (restaurant inside a historic cargo plane from the Iran-Contra era), La Luna at GAIA Hotel (5-star happy hour 4-6pm with live music), El Lagarto (2x1 drinks with sunset views), Barba Roja (tradition since 1975), and El Patio de Cafe Milagro (nightly happy hour 4-6pm with craft beers).

Puerto Viejo

The Caribbean coast offers a completely different vibe - Afro-Caribbean culture, laid-back reggae rhythms, and bohemian spirit. Instead of flashy clubs, find charming beach bars and open-air venues playing live reggae and dancehall. Try a Coco Loco served in a freshly hacked coconut.

Must-visit spots: The Lazy Mon (famous reggae bar with happy hour deals), Salsa Brava (chill by day, DJ party by night), Johnny's Place (live Caribbean music), Luke's Taproom at Kaya's Place ($5 pints of honey mango ale and chocolate porter), and Tasty Waves (Taco Tuesday with $4 tacos and happy hour 4-6pm).

Playas del Coco

By day an unassuming beach town, by night Coco Beach comes alive with energy. Home to the famous Chiliguaro shot - a mix of guaro (local sugar-cane liquor), hot sauce, lime, and salt that was born right here. Large expat community means great sports bars and familiar pub grub.

Must-visit spots: Zi Lounge (live music and friendly vibe), multiple sports bars for catching games, and beachfront cantinas serving the original Chiliguaro recipe alongside cold Imperial beers.

Santa Teresa

This bohemian beach paradise on the Nicoya Peninsula draws surfers, yogis, and digital nomads from around the world. The international crowd creates an eclectic happy hour scene with everything from craft cocktails to fresh coconut water spiked with guaro as the Pacific sun sets.

Must-visit spots: Banana Beach Club, La Lora Amarilla, Vista de Olas (sunset views), and Kika Beach (bohemian beachfront vibes with cold Imperial beers).
